Confidentiality
Protection of company and client information
▶

Confidential Information Includes:

  1. Any information relating to the trading position, business, products, services, affairs and finances of the Company including (but not limited to) marketing information and plans, market opportunities, product lists, the Company's financial information, results and forecasts, manpower or expansion plans, the remuneration and benefits paid to the Company's employees and officers, lists of suppliers, agents, consultants, distributors, clients or customers and their needs and requirements, the terms of business with them and the fees and commissions charged to or by them, information relating to prospects and tenders contemplated, offered or undertaken by the Company and any other matters connected with the products or services manufactured, marketed, provided or obtained by the Company.
  2. Any information relating to clients of the Company.
  3. Technical data and know-how relating to the business of the Company or any of its suppliers, agents, consultants, distributors, clients or customers including (but not limited to) product designs and specifications, product lists, ideas, inventions, drawings and plans, research and development, manufacturing processes, techniques, formulae, trade secrets, computer systems and software, costs, margins, prices, production and business methods, business plans and forecasts and any other technical matters connected with the products or services manufactured, marketed, provided or obtained by the Company.
  4. Details of any other employees of the Company.
  5. Any other information concerning the business and affairs of the Company which is not generally known to the public.

Your Obligations

You must not (except in the proper performance of your duties or as authorised by the Company) during or at any time after the termination of your employment use or disclose to any person confidential information. This restriction shall continue to apply after the termination of your employment without limit in time but shall cease to apply to information or knowledge which becomes public knowledge other than through unauthorised disclosure by you.

Document Handling

You acknowledge that all documents, papers, disks or other media containing confidential information and all copies thereof made by you shall remain the property of the Company and upon termination of your employment (or upon demand by the Company) you shall return the same to the Company.

Absence Policy
Comprehensive absence management procedures
▶
Important: This policy applies to all employees and includes formal procedures that may affect your employment.

1. Overview

The Company is committed to promoting good attendance whilst providing appropriate support for employees who are unable to attend work due to sickness or other legitimate reasons. This policy sets out the standards expected and the procedures to follow.

2. If You Know You Will Be Absent

If you know in advance that you will be absent from work, you must notify your manager as soon as possible, ideally giving at least 24 hours' notice. You must provide:

  • The reason for your absence
  • The expected duration
  • Any urgent work that needs to be covered

3. If You Are Sick or Injured

Procedure to follow:
  • Contact your immediate manager by telephone on the first day of absence before your normal start time
  • If you cannot make the call yourself, arrange for someone to call on your behalf
  • Provide details of your illness and expected return date if known
  • Keep in regular contact during extended periods of absence
  • Contact your manager immediately if your return date changes

4. Evidence of Sickness or Injury

Self-certification: For absences of 7 calendar days or less, you must complete a self-certification form upon your return to work.

Medical certificate: For absences longer than 7 calendar days, you must provide a medical certificate from your doctor. Additional medical certificates must be provided for continued absence.

5. Payment for Sickness or Injury

Statutory Sick Pay (SSP) is payable in accordance with current legislation. Company sick pay may be available at the company's discretion and as detailed in your contract of employment.

6. Returning to Work

All employees must participate in a return-to-work interview with their manager following any period of absence. This meeting will:

  • Confirm your fitness to return to work
  • Identify any support needed
  • Discuss any patterns of absence
  • Update absence records

7. Long-term or Persistent Absence

The Company will monitor absence levels and may take formal action where:

  • Absence levels are unacceptable
  • There is a pattern of absence
  • There are concerns about the genuineness of absences
Absence Triggers: The Company uses an absence scoring system. Certain scores may trigger formal absence management procedures including capability meetings.

8. Other Types of Absence

Jury Service

Time off for jury service is granted as required by law. You must provide the jury summons as soon as possible and any payment certificates.

Public Duties

Reasonable time off may be granted for certain public duties such as serving as a magistrate, councillor, or school governor. This is usually unpaid unless specified in your contract.

Emergency Leave

Reasonable unpaid time off may be granted for genuine emergencies involving dependants. This should be used only for unexpected situations that require your immediate attention.

Disciplinary Policy
Formal procedures for addressing conduct and performance
▶

Informal Resolution

Minor issues should first be addressed through informal discussion, coaching, and support.

Formal Procedure

Steps in formal disciplinary process:
  1. Investigation: Gather facts and evidence
  2. Disciplinary Meeting: Employee invited with right to representation
  3. Decision: Outcome communicated in writing within 5 working days
  4. Appeal: Right to appeal within 5 working days of decision

Possible Outcomes

  • No action required
  • Verbal warning
  • Written warning
  • Final written warning
  • Dismissal (with or without notice)

Gross Misconduct

Examples of gross misconduct that may lead to summary dismissal:
  • Theft or fraud
  • Serious breach of confidentiality
  • Violence or threatening behavior
  • Serious breach of health and safety rules
  • Serious negligence causing loss or damage

Right to Representation

You have the right to be accompanied by a colleague or trade union representative at formal disciplinary hearings.

Harassment and Bullying Policy
Prevention measures and reporting procedures
▶

Definition of Harassment

Harassment is unwanted conduct related to protected characteristics that has the purpose or effect of violating dignity or creating an intimidating, hostile, degrading, humiliating, or offensive environment.

Definition of Bullying

Bullying is offensive, intimidating, malicious, or insulting behavior involving the misuse of power that can make a person feel vulnerable, upset, humiliated, undermined, or threatened.

Examples of Unacceptable Behavior

  • Offensive jokes, comments, or gestures
  • Unwanted physical contact or invasion of personal space
  • Displaying or circulating offensive material
  • Exclusion from workplace social activities
  • Verbal or written abuse
  • Threats or intimidation
  • Unfair criticism or impossible deadlines

Reporting Procedure

If you experience harassment or bullying:

  1. Tell the person their behavior is unwelcome (if you feel safe to do so)
  2. Keep records of incidents (dates, witnesses, details)
  3. Report to your manager, HR, or senior management
  4. Formal investigation will be conducted
  5. Support will be provided throughout the process

Investigation and Action

All complaints will be investigated promptly, fairly, and confidentially. Proven cases of harassment or bullying will result in disciplinary action up to and including dismissal.

Support Available

Support includes counseling services, adjustments to working arrangements, and protection from retaliation. External support agencies can also be accessed.
